SEAtile - A Carbon neutral Product
SEAtile is an outcome to a research looking at an Interior product made of seaweed and local materials promoting circular economy that provides better environmental performance than its predecessors.It is a product system change with integration of 3 major sectors through material science for a low impact living. Combining interior architecture , construction and proposed sustainable seaweed farming industry in Australia.

Current proposal suggests re-designing a tile leaving brick as a part of stage II research study.This here is the products life cycle, where the 3 sectors work in sync with each other to create a sustainable circular loop from extraction of raw materials to manufacturing, installation and recycling.

It starts, from gathering of harvested seaweed from the IMTA blue economy seaweed farms Tasmania and Port Philip Bay.


Amongst the many species of seaweed, a few brown ones are used for developing the product at this stage, with a potential for involving all the suitable species of seaweed.Next is MANUFACTURING where the dried seaweed is used to develop biopolymer activated clay composition in the presence of alkali and alginate. The stabilised clay rearranges the dissolved atoms in a bare minimum heat between 80 to 100 degree Celsius to form a strong dense structure. The clay composition thus formed can be extruded to shape the tile with absolutely no firing required.At the end of its life cycle , it can be possibly recycled by disintegration and crushing. through the biological system. The separated seaweed fibers are utilized as biochar, fertilizers or cattle feed and the clay gets technically recycled to be used again as a raw material (currently based on hypothesis as COVID19 has resulted in insufficient material testing). Apart from being 100% recyclable, SEAtile has the ability to capture carbon in the home setting while providing a sensory sea experience through its visual and tactile qualities.
